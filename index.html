The Five Milestones of the Iphone

  • iPhone 1st generation

    • The Apple company is the second most valuable global brand. According to Statista, in 2021, 46.9% of users use an iPhone. The company has $195.57 billion in cash and as every new product is released they get billions more. Apple created phones, tablets, wireless ear buds, laptops and more but where did this all start from? The first mobile created by Apple Inc. is called, iPhone and was released June 29, 2007. The creator of this multi-billion invention is Ceo Steve Jobs. The purpose of the iPhone was to text and call.

    It's a 4.5 x 2.4 x 0.46 in. model and weighs 4.8oz. It has a 2-megapixel resolution camera with 5 functional buttons. The first button is the power button located on the top of the phone. The home button is at the bottom to exit an app that you are using. In newer versions of the iPhone the power button and home button, while held together, takes a screenshot of whatever page you are on. iPhone 1st generation can play audios and you can control the volume by using the 2 volume buttons; one to increase the volume and one to decrease. The last button on the device was a silent button. That button you could switch to turn your ringer sound off or on. It is located on the side, over the volume buttons. The Smartphone had 16 apps. The main ones being SMS Text, Camera, Youtube, Phone, Mail and Safari.

    Prices for the iPhone were usually 2 prices. iPhone 4GB was $499 and the 8GB, $599. There has been about 6.1 million sales from when it came out. On the Apple website they no longer support the phone or sell it.

  • iPhone 6

    • The next revolutionary product from Apple is the iPhone 6 It upgraded from a small device that could only text and call to a device that can let you do more than that. The iPhone 6 came out September 25, 2015. It came in 3 colors: Space Grey, Silver and Gold. What was different about the iPhone 6 was the features. Not only could you text and call but you could download and play games. With the design of the iPhone 1st generation, the phone was about the size of the palm of your hand. Now, with the upgrade the iPhone 6 is 4.7 inches with a 16:9 resolution of 1334x750. There was a drastic change in speed and storage between the iPhone and the iPhone 6.

    Not only was there 1 version of the phone, there are 2 other versions. Theses newer versions came out in 2015.

  • iPhone X

    • iPhone X was meant to be the phone of the future. Released on September 12th, 2017, this phone would be the example for other phone brand companies. A few things made this phone futuristic was that it was all display which gave users more space to use. With the newer previous phones, there had been a display around the screen either white or black. It also has a new face ID recognition that users had never seen before. The 7 megapixel TrueDepth made face recognition possible. According to Pocket-lint, TrueDepth camera system will detect your face with a flood illuminator. A flood illuminator produces infrared lights to illuminate of your face. With that the camera will take an image on dot projector will project out over 30,000 infrared dots. Animoji, a new feature, allows you to play around with emojis. iPhone X recognizes your facial movements and from there you can speak with them.

    We have a huge upgrade with the specific details of the device. It's screen time is 21 hours compared to both the iPhone 6 and iPhone first generation. It weighs 6.14 oz. which is less than the iPhone 6 Plus. It has increased in storage and can now hold 256GB of data. No iPhone before this had ever had a dual lens as good quality as the iPhone X. The plus versions of iPhone 6-8 had dual camera and any other version didn't. Dual cameras have 2 sensors instead of one. There are 3 other iPhone X models: iPhone XR, iPhone XS, and iPhone XS Max. To find more information of these models you can go to the Apple website.

  • iPhone 11

    • Recently made iPhone 11 was released on September 20th, 2019. This is the phone most people have today. In 2020 there was reported 16 million sales. For the most part, Apple did not make phones in other colors than grey, black, silver, rose gold, and gold. iPhone 11 has colors that would be appealing to users: red, yellow, green, purple, white and black.

    This phone has 2 cameras, similar to the iPhone 7 and iPhone 8. From the Apple website, "The dual-camera system lets users easily zoom between each camera while Audio Zoom matches the audio to the video framing for more dynamic sound.... to videos instantly all within the new, easy-to-use Camera interface." With this new phone you can take pictures in the dark and still get quality pictures. Similar to the iPhone X, it has Face ID recognition. This feature is 30% faster as well as the airdropping feature. Airdropping is when you can send pictures, videos, contacts and more to someone without having their number. IOS 7 and up support this and was made July 20 2011.

  • iPhone 12

    • The 5th milestone for now is the iPhone 12. It came out not to long ago, October 23, 2020. It has a super retina XDR display "6.1". This phone has more a boxier look to it than the pervious iPhones because its edges are made it aluminum. Phones like the iPhone X, 11 and 6 plus weigh over 6 ounces. With this phone it was created to be the lightest 5G phone. Alike the iPhone 11, there are a variety of colors to pick from: black, white, red, green, blue and the newest, purple.

    There isn't that much that changed with the device other than battery life. The hours of battery life is increasing with every new iPhone. The iPhone 12 can last 16 days while the iPhone 11 can only do 60 hours. There's an iPhone 12 Pro and Pro Max selling for $1,000. As time goes past older iPhones decrease in value. A phone that would've been $800 is most likely $500 or $400.

    Many apple users were angry about what is inside the iPhone 12 box. Apple took out the lightning connector and lighting power cord, Reason being carbon emissions. This caused a negative reaction because most people wanted a new charger. According to the article Examining the Carbon Footprint of Devices by Srilatha, it states that, "Manufacturing a smartphone accounts for 85%-95% of the annual carbon footprint of the phone, and the average smartphone requires 55 Kg of CO2e to manufacture". We can see why Apple would want to decrease their emissions. Although many were angered the iPhone 12 still got 100 million unit sales.
